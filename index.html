‚ö†Ô∏è Mobile Constraints
Mobile computing constraints = the disadvantages caused by battery life and computing power vs power consumption.
üîãConstraint 1: Battery Life

The more power a CPU uses, the more instructions it can complete ‚Äî but smartphones have limited battery that must last all day.

Desktop CPUs can draw 100+ watts. Smartphone CPUs? Just 2-5 watts. This limits performance drastically.

üî•Constraint 2: Power vs Heat

More power = more heat. Desktop CPUs use heat sinks and fans to cool down. Smartphones? No space for cooling fans!

If a smartphone CPU ran at desktop speeds, it would overheat and catch fire. So mobile chips are intentionally slower to stay cool.

üí° Fun Fact: A high-end gaming PC uses more power in ONE day than a high-end smartphone uses in an ENTIRE year!
üìµ Case Study: Samsung Galaxy Note 7 (2016)

The Galaxy Note 7 was one of 2016's fastest and most expensive smartphones. A few weeks after launch, Samsung received thousands of complaints that phones were heating up to dangerous levels.

Result? 2 million phones recalled. After testing 200,000 phones, Samsung found the problem: lack of space between the CPU and battery. The CPU's heat warped the battery connectors, causing them to short circuit and catch fire.

Airlines banned the Note 7. Production stopped completely. All because of heat management failure!

üîå Other Mobile Limitations
üìêScreen Size

Even the biggest smartphone screen is tiny compared to a desktop monitor. This makes spreadsheets, word processing and multitasking difficult. Smartphones can't display large amounts of information well.

‚å®Ô∏èInput Method

On-screen touch keyboards are great for quick messages but not for entering large amounts of text. Physical keyboard + mouse is still superior for productivity work.

üì¶Integrated Components

A desktop CPU is just a CPU. A smartphone chip contains: CPU, GPU, LTE modem, Wi-Fi, RAM, Bluetooth, GPS ‚Äî all in one! Each component must be smaller and less powerful.

‚ö° The Bottom Line: Smartphones are incredible for mobility and location-based tasks. Desktops are unbeatable for processing power and serious work. Choose the right tool for the job!
‚ö° Unit 3.2: Performance Factors
What makes one computer faster than another? Six key components determine performance. Click each to learn more!
üß†
CPU
The brain of the computer. Two factors matter: (1) Speed (GHz) ‚Äî how many instructions per second, and (2) Multiprocessing ‚Äî handling tasks from multiple apps simultaneously. More cores = better multitasking.
üéÆ
GPU
Graphics Processing Unit. Affects graphic-intensive tasks like gaming and video editing. Two factors: GPU clock speed (MHz) and GPU type. A slow GPU won't affect browsing or documents, but games will look terrible!
üíæ
RAM
When RAM is too small, the computer slows down ‚Äî constantly swapping data in and out. When switching between apps, if RAM is full, your computer might auto-close apps to free memory. More RAM = smoother multitasking.
‚ö°
Cache Memory
CPU Cache: High-speed memory built into the CPU for quick data access. Disk Cache: Small RAM in the hard drive storing data being sent/received. Web Cache: Stores images/pages on your HDD so you can reload previously-viewed pages faster.
üíø
Storage Speed
How fast your HDD/SSD reads and writes data. Affects: opening programs, loading large files, booting your OS. HDD: measured in RPM (5400, 7200). SSD: much faster! Disk caching also helps speed up repeated reads/writes.
üåê
Network Speed
The NIC (Network Interface Card) connects your PC to the internet. Measured in Mbps: 10 Mbps = slow, 100 Mbps = faster, 1000 Mbps (1 Gbps) = fastest. Affects loading webpages, downloading games/updates, streaming video, copying data over network.
üîß Pro Tip: A computer is only as fast as its slowest component! A new CPU won't help if you have 2GB of ancient RAM and a 5400 RPM hard drive from 2010.

üöåBonus: The Bus System

The bus system connects the CPU with memory, input/output devices and storage. Think of it as the electrical highway carrying data between components. Faster buses = faster communication = better performance!
